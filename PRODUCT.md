# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Decision-makers at German Mittelstand (SMB) companies — owners, office/ops leads — who know their business runs on analog processes (Excel, paper, fax) and are evaluating a digitalization partner. They're wary of oversized agencies with heavy overhead and slow, impersonal engagements. They want a partner who talks plainly, understands their day-to-day operations, and can show concrete before/after results, not slideware.

## Product Purpose

Marketing site for IOFDigital, a Gummersbach-based digital agency (operating under ProConstruct) offering four services: IT & Microsoft 365, Automatisierung & Power Platform, Business Solutions, and Design & Multimedia. The site's job is to build enough trust and clarity that a visiting Mittelstand decision-maker books an "Erstgespräch" (initial consultation). Success = qualified consultation bookings, not raw traffic or vanity metrics.

## Brand Personality

Warm, approachable, human — while staying direct and pragmatic (existing tagline: "Digital. Direkt. Durchdacht."). Expert but relatable: speaks "auf Augenhöhe" (at eye level) with business owners, not down to them. Confident without being corporate-cold or flashy-startup. Proof-driven over hype-driven.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS/agency templates: stock-photo handshakes, corporate blue-gradient boilerplate, cookie-cutter icon-grid feature sections.
- Cold enterprise-consultancy tone (McKinsey-slide sterility).
- Flashy Silicon-Valley startup bravado (buzzwords, hero-metric templates, gradient-text hype).
- Anything that reads as impersonal or templated — the whole point is "not a big faceless agency."

## Design Principles

1. **Speak plainly, prove with numbers.** The site's credibility rests on concrete data (DESI ranking, fax-usage stats, time-savings) and named real clients — keep this evidentiary backbone central, don't dilute it with vague marketing claims.
2. **Show, don't sell.** In-house products (Hermes, ClockIt) and named clients (Frechdachs Jugendhilfe, ProConstruct) are proof of competence — let them carry weight rather than adjectives.
3. **Warmth over corporate polish.** Even as visual craft goes up-market, the voice stays human and direct, never stiff or jargon-heavy.
4. **One clear action per screen.** Every section should visibly funnel toward "Erstgespräch vereinbaren" — no competing or decorative CTAs.
5. **Respect the reader's time.** Dense information (services, stats, products) delivered cleanly; no filler copy, no restated headlines.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA contrast. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for the scroll-hijacked narrative section and the WebGL light-ray shaders (fall back to a static state, no forced motion). No other special requirements specified.
